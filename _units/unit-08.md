# Unit 8

## Reflection
### What?
Unit 8 focused on how artificial neural networks learn through backpropagation, gradient descent, and iterative weight updates. The unit moved beyond neural network structure and examined how error is calculated, how gradients guide optimization, and how learning rate and epochs affect convergence. I also completed the gradient descent exercise and reviewed the seminar discussion on explainability in ANN applications.
### So what?
This unit helped me understand that neural network training is a controlled optimization process rather than trial and error. The gradient descent exercise made this especially clear. A learning rate of 0.08 converged quickly, 0.01 was much slower, and 0.5 caused divergence. That showed me how sensitive training is to hyperparameter choice. The seminar also reinforced that strong predictive performance is not enough on its own if a model cannot be explained or defended in regulated settings.
### Now what?
Going forward, I want to pay more attention to optimization settings, convergence behavior, and model transparency when working with neural networks, rather than focusing only on final accuracy.
