# Unit 5

## Reflection
### What?
Unit 5 focused on clustering as an unsupervised learning method for grouping similar data points. The unit covered segmentation, similarity and distance measures, K means clustering, and ways to evaluate clusters such as SSE, the elbow method, and silhouette scores. I also completed the wiki task on clustering animations and the Jaccard coefficient exercise.
### So what?
This unit showed me that clustering can be useful for exploratory segmentation, but its results depend heavily on modelling choices. The animation task made it clear that K means is sensitive to centroid initialization and can impose clusters even when the data has weak natural structure. The Jaccard exercise also helped me understand that similarity depends on how the data is encoded and which measure is appropriate for the data type.
### Now what?
Going forward, I want to be more critical when interpreting clustering outputs, especially in noisy or weakly structured data. Unit 5 showed me that choosing the similarity measure, the number of clusters, and the evaluation method can change the result substantially, so these decisions need to be justified clearly.
