# Unit 10

## Reflection
### What?
Unit 10 introduced Natural Language Processing as a major machine learning area and focused on how deep learning, especially Transformer-based models, is used for language tasks. The unit also covered evaluation and deployment issues, while the seminar and activity continued the deep learning strand through a CNN tutorial and CNN Explainer exercise linked to the upcoming CIFAR-10 assignment.
### So what?
This unit helped me see that deep learning is not only about architecture choice, but also about evaluation, monitoring, and understanding model limits. The CNN Explainer activity made that practical. A sports car image was misclassified as lifeboat, and an out-of-distribution landscape image was confidently labelled as school bus. That showed me that softmax confidence does not guarantee correctness, especially when the model must force an unfamiliar input into a fixed label set.
### Now what?
Going forward, I want to be more critical when interpreting deep learning outputs, especially where confidence scores look persuasive. Unit 10 reinforced that I need to evaluate models across training, validation, and test settings, and to discuss trade-offs and limitations rather than focusing only on predictions.
